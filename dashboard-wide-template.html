The pace line shows how far above or below your expected pace you are — not total spend. Land exactly on the expected line and it plots flat at zero; under-pace dips down (green), over-pace climbs up (yellow → red).

Weekly pacing is seasonal: on the weekly card, “expected” is your typical weekly rhythm, not a flat around-the-clock burn. We build a completion-factor curve from your last few complete weeks — the share of a week’s total you’d normally have spent by this point — so a busy Monday morning isn’t judged as if nights and weekends burned at the same rate, and the projected cap-hit follows your real shape. It needs ~3 weeks of history to switch on; before that (and on the 5-hour and monthly cards) the expected line stays a straight, steady-burn pace to the limit.

Spending limit: Anthropic doesn't publish a hard dollar number for Max tiers, so we work it out from /api/oauth/usage — the same feed Claude Code's /usage command reads. If that endpoint is unreachable, we fall back to the observed ceiling (~$5,900/week).

Reset time: pulled live from /api/oauth/usage. The reset anchor is usually stable but Anthropic can shift it (bug fixes, billing changes), so we always trust the live timestamp over a day-of-week assumption.

What it shows: the sessions you ran in the chosen window whose peak context hit at least 500K, ranked by how heavy they got. Anything lighter than 500K is filtered out so the list stays focused on the ones where doing something would actually matter. The action column suggests what to do based on peak context:

  • ok — under 200K, nothing to do
  • monitor — 200K–500K, context is starting to build
  • consider — 500K–800K, line up a /compact soon
  • compact now — 800K–950K, getting hot, /compact is still safe here
  • save & reset — over 950K, near the 1M wall; /compact can drop important early context this close to the edge. Better play: /save a summary, start a new session, and pick up from that summary.

Starting fresh beats compacting when you're near the 1M wall, when you've already compacted recently (less to strip), or when the chat has accumulated dead-end explorations you don't need to carry forward.

The "last turn" column tells you how recent the session is — just now means live right now; older entries help you spot which finished sessions burned the most so you know where to compact, save, or restructure similar work next time.

Session IDs here are the first 8 characters of the Claude Code session filenames under ~/.claude/projects/.

What it shows: the chart is daily or weekly aggregates over your lookback. The strip above is a quick read on current pace: either 7-day averages vs the week before, or a rolling 24-hour snapshot vs the same prior-week daily baseline.

Reading the trend: bars show individual days (or weeks, if you toggle Week); the line is a smoothed running average. Dashed markers point to capability events (1M context turning on, Opus launches, Fable 5 launching) — useful anchors for where habits often shift.

Tips

How to spend less:

  • avg ctx, cost/turn — /compact more often, Grep before Read, hand research to sub-agents so their context doesn't pile onto yours.
  • cost/day, turns/day — batch related questions into one prompt; move mechanical work from Opus to Sonnet.
  • cache hit % — above 85% is healthy; below 70% means the session keeps reshaping context each turn.
  • frontier share — Opus/Fable over 70% is expensive. Sonnet is cheaper per token for mechanical work at similar quality.
